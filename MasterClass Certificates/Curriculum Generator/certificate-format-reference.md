# MasterClass Certificate Format Reference

This document defines the canonical structure for MasterClass Certificate curriculum output. Use this as the schema when generating new certificate curricula.

---

## Full Certificate Structure

### 1. Certificate 1-Liner
A single sentence capturing the transformation. Should be aspirational and specific.

**Pattern**: `[Action verb] yourself with [what you'll learn] to [professional outcome].`

**Examples from live certs**:
- "The Moonshot Factory, Google's Innovation Lab, reveals the tools and frameworks to drive innovation, navigate uncertainty, and turn big ideas into real-world impact."
- "Lead with clarity and calm using battle-tested leadership principles trusted by Navy SEALs."

---

### 2. Essential Details
Always include these four items verbatim:

1. **Shareable Certificate**: Earn a certificate that you can add to your resume and LinkedIn profile.
2. **For Beginners**: No prior knowledge required.
3. **Flexible Schedule**: Complete videos & projects at your own pace.
4. **15 Hours to Complete**: 6 weeks at 2–3 hours a week.

---

### 3. Real Outcomes For Your Career
Header: **Real Outcomes For Your Career**

5 bullet points. Each outcome should be:
- Career-specific (not generic)
- Action-oriented (what you can DO after)
- Grounded in the subject domain

**Example (AI certificate)**:
- Gain a robust understanding of AI fundamentals and advanced concepts.
- Develop practical skills for applying AI in various professional contexts.
- Enhance your ability to analyze and solve real-world problems using AI.
- Achieve professional recognition and advancement through a comprehensive understanding of AI.
- Equip yourself with the knowledge to innovate and lead AI projects.

---

### 4. Skills You'll Gain
Header: **Skills You'll Gain**

5–7 short skill labels (2–4 words each), displayed as pills/tags.

**Example (AI certificate)**:
- AI Fundamentals
- Machine Learning
- Data Analysis
- Ethical AI Practices
- AI Integration in Business
- Problem Solving with AI
- AI Project Management

---

### 5. Curriculum Overview
Header: **Certificate Program Overview**

One paragraph (3–5 sentences) describing:
- Program length and structure (6 weeks, 15 hours)
- Who it's designed for (busy professionals)
- What the learner experience is like (mix of theory + hands-on)
- The unique focus/framing of this certificate

---

### 6. Curriculum Modules (6 total)

Each module follows this exact format:

```
#### Module [N]: [Title]

- **Duration**: 1 Week (2.5 hours)
- **What You'll Learn**: [1–2 sentence description of what this module covers]
- **Videos**:
  - [Video title] ([X] min)
  - [Video title] ([X] min)
  - [Video title] ([X] min)
  - [Video title] ([X] min)
  - [Video title] ([X] min)
- **Activities**:
  [Activity name]: [Brief description of the interactive exercise] (30 min).
- **Supplemental Reading**:
  - *[Reading title]* ([X] min)
  - *[Reading title]* ([X] min)
- **Skills Included**:
  [Skill name(s)]
```

**Module timing guidelines**:
- Each video: 4–5 minutes
- 5 videos per module = ~20–23 min total
- 1 activity per module = 30 min
- 2 supplemental readings = 25 min total
- Total per module: ~75–80 min (rounds to 2.5 hours)

**Module arc guidelines**:
- Module 1: Foundations/Introduction — "What is this and why does it matter?"
- Module 2: Core Framework — "How does it work?"
- Module 3: Application — "How do I use this?"
- Module 4: Advanced/Real-World — "How do I handle complexity?"
- Module 5: Leadership/Strategy — "How do I scale or lead this?"
- Module 6: Future/Capstone — "Where does this go, and how do I demonstrate mastery?"

## Live Certificate Examples

For current tone and style reference, see:
- **Breakthrough Innovation Strategy**: `https://www.masterclass.com/certificates/breakthrough-innovation-strategy-from-bold-idea-to-business-impact`
- **Leadership & Decision-Making Under Pressure**: `https://www.masterclass.com/certificates/leadership-and-decision-making-under-pressure`

Key style observations from live certs:
- Modules have bold, action-oriented titles (e.g., "Innovate at a 10X scale", "Blueprint the impossible")
- Learning objectives connect to tangible deliverables (blueprints, pitches, frameworks)
- Activities simulate real work (not quizzes) — e.g., "Build your Moonshot Blueprint", "Draft an intent statement"
- Tone is aspirational but practical: learners are treated as capable professionals, not students
