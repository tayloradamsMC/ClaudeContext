# Partner Pitch Framing Guide

Use this guide when generating the "why partner" rationale in Section 1 of a certificate curriculum pitch. The goal is to make the case that this specific partner × this specific subject × MasterClass is an obvious win for all three parties.

---

## What Makes a Strong Partner Fit

A good partner has at least 3 of these 5 signals:

1. **Brand authority in a skill domain** — Their name is already associated with excellence in the subject area (e.g., Goldman Sachs → finance, Nike → performance, McKinsey → strategy)
2. **Aspirational audience** — Their customers/employees want to grow professionally and associate with the brand's identity
3. **Credentialing gap** — Their audience needs verifiable proof of skill, but current options are too academic, too generic, or too expensive
4. **Business alignment** — The subject area supports their products, services, or talent strategy (e.g., a tech company offering a coding certificate to drive developer adoption)
5. **Distribution advantage** — They have an existing audience, platform, or relationship that can activate learners (e.g., HR platforms, alumni networks, customer bases)

---

## The "Why Partner" Rationale — What to Explain

For each partner, answer these four questions in the pitch:

### 1. Why is this partner credible on this subject?
What does their brand stand for that makes a certificate from them meaningful? What expertise do they have (employees, products, methodology, history) that gives the certificate legitimacy?

### 2. Why does their audience need this?
What is the skill gap, career transition, or professional challenge their audience faces? Why is this the right moment for this certificate?

### 3. Why can't they do this without MasterClass?
What does MasterClass uniquely bring — world-class instructors, production quality, aspirational brand, proven learning format — that the partner can't replicate on their own?

### 4. What does each party get?
- **Partner**: Brand extension, audience engagement, talent development, potential revenue, differentiation
- **Learner**: Career-relevant skills, credible credential, access to world-class knowledge
- **MasterClass**: New audience reach, brand association, distribution

## Framing by Partner Type

### Technology Companies (e.g., Salesforce, Google, Microsoft, Stripe)
- **Subject areas that fit**: AI/ML, product management, data analysis, growth strategy, emerging tech
- **Why they're credible**: They live and breathe these domains; their products are built on these skills
- **Audience motivation**: Developers, admins, and power users want to grow into leadership roles; the certificate validates their expertise with their employer
- **Value prop framing**: "Customers who are more skilled use our platform better, advocate more, and churn less"

### Financial Institutions (e.g., Goldman Sachs, Fidelity, JPMorgan, Visa)
- **Subject areas that fit**: Corporate finance, investment analysis, negotiation, financial modeling, risk management
- **Why they're credible**: They are the gold standard in these domains; a certificate "by Goldman" carries enormous weight
- **Audience motivation**: Finance professionals, MBAs, career-switchers, and high earners want credentials that signal elite-level thinking
- **Value prop framing**: "A Goldman × MasterClass certificate is the aspirational credential the market has been waiting for"

### Leadership & Management Orgs (e.g., BCG, McKinsey, Deloitte, Korn Ferry)
- **Subject areas that fit**: Strategic planning, team leadership, executive communication, negotiation, change management
- **Why they're credible**: Their methodologies and frameworks already define best practices in the field
- **Audience motivation**: Managers and executives want to formalize and validate frameworks they already use implicitly
- **Value prop framing**: "We codify your firm's methodology into a certificate the market can access"

### Consumer/Lifestyle Brands (e.g., Nike, Peloton, Patagonia)
- **Subject areas that fit**: Performance mindset, leadership under pressure, innovation, sustainability strategy
- **Why they're credible**: Their brand identity is built around transformation, excellence, and aspiration
- **Audience motivation**: Their customers don't just buy products — they buy identity; a certificate deepens that relationship
- **Value prop framing**: "This extends your brand promise from lifestyle into professional development"

### Media & Publishing (e.g., HBR, WSJ, Bloomberg, The Economist)
- **Subject areas that fit**: Data literacy, strategic communication, economic thinking, negotiation, leadership
- **Why they're credible**: They are trusted authorities on business and economic thinking
- **Audience motivation**: Readers want to go beyond passive consumption and actively develop the skills they read about
- **Value prop framing**: "Turn your readership into a learning community"

---

## Subject-Partner Fit Signals

A subject area has strong fit with a partner when:

- The partner **already produces content** in this domain (articles, courses, reports, toolkits)
- The partner's **employees are known experts** in this area
- The subject area **supports their commercial goals** (e.g., a CRM company + sales strategy)
- The partner's **audience actively asks for** this type of guidance
- The subject gives the partner a **talent or recruitment advantage** (certificate as talent magnet)
- The certificate would be **shared by learners in contexts** where the partner wants visibility (LinkedIn, resumes, job applications)

---

## Language Patterns to Use

**Opening the rationale**:
- "[Partner] has spent [X] years building [expertise] — this certificate turns that into a credential the market can access."
- "For [Partner]'s audience, [subject area] isn't a nice-to-have — it's the skill gap standing between them and the next level."
- "A [Partner] × MasterClass certificate on [subject] would be the first credentialing option in this space that's both aspirational and accessible."

**Articulating MasterClass's value**:
- "MasterClass brings what [Partner] can't build alone: world-class production, an aspirational brand learners trust, and a format proven to convert intent into completion."
- "This isn't a webinar or a PDF. It's a [X]-module, [Y]-hour learning experience that feels like [Partner] at its best."

**Closing the case**:
- "This is a category-creating move for [Partner] — and for MasterClass, it opens [audience segment] at scale."
- "The question isn't whether [Partner]'s audience needs this. It's whether we move first."
